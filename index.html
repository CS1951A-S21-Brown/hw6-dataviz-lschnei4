1. Describe how your dashboard answers the questions presented:
 My first graph gives top 10 genres by number of titles in that genre. Viewers are able to toggle between TV Shows or Movies.  My second graph displays the average duration in minutes for reccent movies. Once again, my graph would have been too cluttered if I had shown every year in the dataset, so I decided to only display information for reccent years which I define as 1980 and later.
 My third graph shows the top (most common) director and actor pairs for movies released in the United States. When processing the dataset, I noticed that there were some cases of movies that had been released in multiple languages and thus the director and actor pairs had a high number of movies created together. In order to address this, I filtered the data to only show movies released in the United States which got rid of those cases.

Accessible Data Visualization:

 4. Evaluate the accessibility of your dashboard based on the readings in the “Before you begin” section. What kinds of users might find this dashboard accessible and who might have more difficulty? What additional actions might you take to make this dashboard more accessible to all audiences?
 Based on the readings in the "Before you begin" section, I was really conscious of my color choices and tried to use larger fonts. I originally chose a green color scheme, but as the "A Comprehensive Guide to Accessible Data Visualization" article explained, most forms of colorblindness involve green. Thus I chose purples and blues so that my dashboard would be more accessible for colorblind users. I also used larger font sizes, bold text, and thick bar graphs for people with low vision. To improve the accessibility as well, I chose bar graphs which are easier to interpret, separated the boxes with whitespace, andnprovided counts when possible for asssistive technologies to read. That being said, as the second article noted, hover overlays are not compatible with assistive technologies and thus this feature of my dashboard is not very accessible. Also, people with light sensitivity or Achromatopsia might find the colors of the visualization challenging to distinguish.   

 
5. Reflect on the stages of your design and implementation process when you could have taken steps to make your dashboard more accessible to all audiences. What are some factors that kept you from taking these steps?
 In the formatting of my graphs, I could have added a caption or textbox describing the visualization that an AT would be able to read.
